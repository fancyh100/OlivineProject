Metamorphic Rock

Metamorphic rocks are made by the existing rocks types, and the process called metamorphism. Metamorphic rocks make up a large part of Earth's crust and are classified by texture, chemical and mineral assemblage. They can form from tectonic processes such as continental collisions, which cause horizontal pressure, friction and distortion. They are also formed when rock is heated up by the intrusion of hot molten rock called magma from the Eearth's interior.

There are two basic types of metamorphic rocks. Foliated metamorphic rocks such as gneiss, phyllite, schist, and slate have a layered or banded appearance that is produced by exposure to heat and directed pressure.

Non-foliated metamorphic rocks such as hornfels, marble, quartzite, and novaculite do not have a layered or banded appearance.

Classification of metamorphic rock

Metamorphic rock are classified on the basis of texture and mineral composition. The texture can be foliated or non-foliated. Foliated metamorphic rocks are appeared banded or layered. Foliated rocks can be ordered in terms of increasing metamorphism. Non-foliated metamorphic rock usually contains one mineral.
